# Analyzing-Supplier-Performance-Data-for-Maersk

# Introduction
A.P. Moller – Maersk is one of the world’s most influential integrated logistics and supply chain companies, headquartered in Copenhagen and operating since 1904.
Over more than a century, Maersk has evolved from a traditional shipping line into a global end‑to‑end logistics powerhouse Operating in 130+ countries and managing one of the largest container shipping fleets globally. Maersk plays a central role in international trade and global commerce

# Problem Statement
Currently, Maersk faces challenges with inconsistent supplier data, unreliable lead times, delayed deliveries, incomplete procurement records, and limited visibility into supplier defects and order fulfilment performance.

# Project Objective
This project will provide Maersk with a comprehensive analysis of supplier performance, delivery reliability, lead-time variability, defect rates, and procurement data quality. 
By cleaning and analyzing supplier data from procurement, shipment, warehouse, invoice, and quality control systems, the project will help identify unreliable suppliers, reduce operational delays, improve supplier accountability, strengthen procurement decisions, and enhance supply chain performance.

The project will:
- build a comprehensive dashboard that will help Maersk answer important business questions concerning their suppliers and logistics operations, monitor critical KPIs, and optimize their operations.
- improve Maersk ability to analyze and monitor trends in the lead time variance, supplier reliability, and shipping efficiency
- enable Maersk to make data-driven decisions to enhance supplier performance in terms of on-time supplier delivery and reduce defect rates in supplies

The final insights will support Maersk in building a more reliable, data-driven supplier management process that improves delivery performance, reduces defects, strengthens operational visibility, and minimizes supply chain disruptions.

# Key Insights
- Maersk’s supplier performance shows significant reliability and quality gaps, with only 37.2% of items delivered on time (vs. 95–98% industry benchmark) and an average defect rate of 2.52%.
- Supplier average ratings remain moderate (3.0), and the average lead‑time variance (2 days) indicates inconsistent fulfilment
- A small group of suppliers and product categories disproportionately drive delays and defects
- A few high performers demonstrate that strong service levels are achievable

 # Recommendations
 ## Critical:
 - Raise On Time Delivery (OTD) from 37.2% to ≥90% within two quarters through contractual enforcement and supplier development.
 - Enforce SLA penalties for chronic late deliveries, starting with Primesteel and Cassablanca.
 - Launch a corrective action plan with Cape Logistics due to defect rates exceeding 20% of volume.
 - Prioritize root cause analysis on the Industry Supplies category, which accounts for 26.5% of total defects.

## High:
- Implement automated dashboard alerts for OTD below 80% or defect rates above 5% over a rolling three-month period.
- Consider onboarding new suppliers with strong on-time delivery, high acceptance rates, and low defect levels.
- Increase volume allocation to high-performing suppliers such as Dar Machinery Parts, Sahara Packaging, Casablanca Components, and Kigali Precision Tools.
- Tier suppliers into Strategic, Transactional, and Underperforming categories with performance-based contracts.

## Medium:
Differentiate safety stock levels based on lead time variability, with higher buffers for suppliers such as Accra Agro and Mombasa Electricals.

# Conclusion and Next Steps
- Finalize supplier segmentation and agree on the criteria with Procurement and Operations.
- Launch supplier scorecards and share first set of performance reports with top 10 suppliers.
- Initiate corrective action plans with Cape Logistics and other high‑defect or low‑On Time Delivery suppliers.
- Reallocate a portion of volume to Dar Machinery Parts, Sahara Packaging, Cassablanca Components, and Kigali Precision Tools.
